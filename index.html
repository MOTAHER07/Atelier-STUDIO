Modern

Clean lines, neutral palettes, and a focus on open spaces define the modern aesthetic. Less is more, with every element serving both form and function.

Key Characteristics

  • Open floor plans
  • Neutral color palettes
  • Sleek furniture silhouettes
  • Minimal ornamentation
  • Natural light emphasis

Minimalist

Stripped to the essentials, minimalist design celebrates simplicity, empty space, and the beauty of carefully chosen objects. Every item has purpose.

Key Characteristics

  • Monochromatic palettes
  • Hidden storage solutions
  • Statement single pieces
  • Texture over pattern
  • Breathing room between elements

Classic

Timeless elegance with rich materials, symmetrical layouts, and traditional craftsmanship. Classic interiors exude warmth, sophistication, and permanence.

Key Characteristics

  • Symmetrical arrangements
  • Rich wood tones
  • Crown molding and wainscoting
  • Layered textiles
  • Antique and vintage accents

Industrial

Raw, unfinished charm with exposed structural elements, reclaimed materials, and a warehouse-inspired aesthetic that celebrates authenticity.

Key Characteristics

  • Exposed brick and ductwork
  • Metal and iron accents
  • Reclaimed wood surfaces
  • Open shelving
  • Edison bulb lighting
